Labor Day

History, rights and current situation

Labor Day is celebrated on May 1st to honor the struggle of workers for better working conditions, such as the 8-hour workday.

Learn more about Labor Day

Historical aspect

This date originates from workers' protests in Chicago in 1886, where they demanded a reduction in working hours.

Legal aspect

Over time, important labor laws were established to protect workers' rights, such as minimum wage, paid vacations, and workplace safety.

Gender and work

Today, there is a strong focus on gender equality in the workplace, promoting equal rights and opportunities for everyone.

Current situation

Nowadays, work has evolved with technology, remote jobs, and new forms of employment, creating new challenges and rights.
